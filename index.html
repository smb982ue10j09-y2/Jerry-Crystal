Problem & Solution

Modern underwater operations demand scientific precision—accurate sampling and inspection without disturbing delicate aquatic environments. However, this requirement fundamentally conflicts with the limitations of conventional methods.

The Challenge

Societies invest significantly in water monitoring annually, yet current methods fail to achieve precise ecological conservation. Underwater operations face challenges in deep-sea access, accuracy, and minimal environmental disturbance.

Existing alternatives—manned diving, fixed stations, and unmanned equipment—have severe limitations: high risks, depth constraints, limited coverage, and poor adaptability.

Our Solution

Crabion features a bionic crab-leg structure for stable movement across complex water bottoms, accessing previously unreachable areas. It minimizes environmental disturbance and greatly improves sampling and monitoring accuracy.

The hexapod configuration with 18 degrees of freedom provides unparalleled mobility and precise foot placement for superior stability on unpredictable surfaces.

Market Opportunity

The global underwater robotics market is valued at $4.2 billion in 2024, growing at 12.1% CAGR. Our platform meets critical demands in offshore energy, environmental monitoring, and scientific research for stability, precision, and ecological sensitivity.

Target clients include government agencies, aquaculture enterprises, research institutions, and offshore energy companies.

Product Showcase

Beyond a single-purpose device, we offer a versatile, multi-mission platform. Through a rapid-module-swapping system, it seamlessly adapts to diverse operations.

Modular Multi-Mission Platform

Crabion replicates the biomechanics of crab legs and their gait, enabling navigation through complex terrains with minimal environmental disturbance. This capability grants access to previously unreachable underwater zones.

  • Superior Stability: Bionic crab-leg structure ensures stable movement on complex terrains
  • Minimal Environmental Impact: No disruptive propellers, ensuring data integrity with 90% less sediment disturbance
  • Amphibious Capability: Seamless operation from land to water
  • Proven Modularity: Practical swappable payload system for multi-role functionality
  • High Compatibility: Compatible with various cameras, sensors, and robotic arms

Water Quality Monitoring Module

Equipped with high-precision sensors for measuring dissolved oxygen, temperature, salinity, pH, and turbidity. The module can walk along aquaculture net pen bottoms, creating detailed 3D maps of water parameters. This resolves monitoring blind spots, enabling proactive management to prevent stock mortality, optimize feeding, and ensure sustainable practices.

Pipeline Inspection Module

Integrates HD cameras and manipulator arms for detailed visual inspection of offshore energy pipelines and infrastructure. Legged locomotion allows it to walk steadily across muddy, uneven, or debris-strewn surfaces, grasp onto structures for stationary inspection in currents, and navigate tight spaces with precision. This resolves mission interruption and equipment loss, enabling maintenance in previously inaccessible areas.

Topographic Mapping Module

Features multibeam sonar and high-resolution cameras for creating detailed seabed topography maps. Allows scientists to collect pristine water samples and high-definition imagery of seabed habitats without altering the environment they seek to study, thereby guaranteeing the scientific integrity of long-term monitoring data.
